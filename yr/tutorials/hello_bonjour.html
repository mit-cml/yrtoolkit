The Challenge

Youth Radio ran this story about a women's immigrant mother and how she struggles to connect with other parents because she's not confident with her English.

You're challenged with creating an app that could act as an aid for her and other immigrant parents who need a little extra help in English-speaking situations. We can't wait to see what you come up with!

Click connect your app to start building your app!

Connect your App

1. Connect with your device over wifi

Connect using the companion app so you can see your app on your mobile device.

Click here if you don't have a device and here if you don't have wifi or data available.

2. Download and install the MIT AI2 Companion App on your phone

Download from the Play Store using the QR code below or this link.

If you can't use the Play Store click here.

3. Go through the installation instructions on your phone

After downloading, step though the the instructions to install the Companion app on your device.You need to install the MIT AI2 Companion only once, and then leave it on your phone or tablet for whenever you use App Inventor.

4. Open your app's QR code

Click Connect, then AI Companion.

5. Connect your app with your phone

A QR code similar to this should pop up on your screen.

Either scan the QR code or enter the code in the text box.

Hello Bonjour Translator

1. Introduction

You've started with a screen with a few components already added.

2. Go to Blocks page

Click on the Blocks button on the top right of the screen.

3. Add click handler

Click on translateButton and add when translateButton.Click.

4. Request translation

Click on translateButton and add call YandexTranslate1.RequestTranslation to your when translateButton.Click block.

5. Add empty string

Click on Text and add the " " block to the "languageToTranslateTo" socket of your call YandexTranslate1.RequestTranslation block.

6. Add language codes

Click on your " " block and enter the text, "ru-en".

This is how the codes work.

7. Get text to be translated

Click on the russianText label and connect russianText.Text to the textToTranslate socket of the call YandexTranslate1.RequestTranslation block.

8. Get the translated text

Click on YandexTranslate1 again and drag out the when YandexTranslate1.GotTranslation block.

9. Reset the label

Click on the englishText label and drag out the set englishText.Text to block and connect it to when YandexTranslate1.GotTranslation.

10. Get the translated text

Hover over the red translation button and grab and click on get translation. Connect this block to set englishText.Text to.

11. Try and expand your app!

Try out your app on your companion device! When you press the "Translate" button, does the translated text appear?

Next, go to the "Expand your app" section below and add more features to your app!

Add a TextBox

First, delete the russianText label (you don't need it anymore!).

Click on TextBox and add it to your Viewer.

Go to Blocks.

Click on TextBox1 and add TextBox1.Text to the textToTranslate socket of call YandexTranslate1.RequestTranslation.

Shake to clear screen

Click on Sensors. Click and drag AccelerometerSensor to the Viewer.

Click Blocks.

Click on AccelerometerSensor1 and click and add when AccelerometerSensor1.Shaking to the Viewer.

Right-click or control (ctrl) click on call YandexTranslate1.RequestTranslation and click "Duplicate". Drag your new blocks into when AccelerometerSensor1.Shaking.

Test out your app! You should be able to translate your text by shaking or clicking the "Translate" button.

Use the TextToSpeech component

In Designer, click on Sensors and drag and drop TextToSpeech onto the Viewer. It will show up in the non-visible componenets section.

Click Blocks.

Click on TextToSpeak1 and add call TextToSpeech1.Speak into the when YandexTranslate1.GotTranslation block.

Hover over the red translate button again and click on get translation. Drag it to the "message" socket of call TextToSpeech1.Speak.
