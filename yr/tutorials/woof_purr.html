Hello Woof Purr Tutorial

1. Introduction

Let's make an app that can play a dog or cat sound when an image is pressed.

If you haven't set up your device-app connection yet, click here.

2. Add and rename Label

Drag a button on the screen. Rename it to "LabelQuestion".

3. Update LabelQuestion

In the Properties panel, find FontBold and select the box. Set the FontSize to "40" and the Text to "Cats or Dogs?"

4. Add two HorizontalArrangements

Click on Layout in the Components panel and click and drag two HorizontalArrangements below your Label.

5. Add two Buttons

Click on User Interface and click and drag two Buttons into your top HorizontalArrangement (HorizontalArrangement1). Rename one "ButtonCat" and the other "ButtonDog".

6. Update ButtonCat

Select ButtonCat, and go to the Properties panel. Click Image and select "cat.jpg". Then, erase the words under Text.

7. Update ButtonDog

Select ButtonDog, and go to the Properties panel. Click Image and select "dog.jpg". Then, erase the words under Text.

8. Set width of HorizontalArrangement2

Click on HorizontalArrangement2 and in the Properties panel, set its Width to Fill parent.

9. Add four Labels

In User Interface, add four Labels to the bottom HorizontalArrangement (HorizontalArrangement2). Rename them to "LabelCat", "LabelCatVotes", "LabelDog", and "LabelDogVotes".

10. Set LabelCat

Click LabelCat and go to the Properties panel. Set the Width to "Fill parent" and the Text to "Cat Votes".

11. Set LabelCatVotes, LabelDog, and LabelDogVotes

Click LabelCatVotes and set the Width to "Fill parent" and the Text to "0". Click LabelDog and set the Width to "Fill parent" and the Text to "Dog Votes". Click LabelCatVotes and set the Width to "Fill parent" and the Text to "0".

12. Add two Players

Click Media and drag two Player components to your Viewer. They will show up under "Non-visible components". Rename them to "PlayerCat" and "PlayerDog".

13. Add Player sources

Click PlayerCat and in the Properties panel, click Source and choose "cat.ogg". Next, click on PlayerDog, click Source, and choose "dog.ogg".

14. Add when ButtonCat.Click

Now we'll programming vote buttons. Start by clicking Blocks!

In Blocks, click on ButtonCat and click and drag the when ButtonCat.Click block to Viewer.

15. Add call PlayerCat.Start

Now, click on PlayerCat and click and drag the call PlayerCat.Start block to Viewer and connect it to when ButtonCat.Click.

16. Add set LabelCatVotes.Text to

Click on LabelCatVotes and click and drag the set LabelCatVotes.Text to block to Viewer and connect it to when ButtonCat.Click.

17. Add __ + __

Click on Math and click and drag the __ + __ block to set LabelCatVotes.Text to.

18. Add LabelCatVotes.Text

Click on LabelCatVotes and click and drag the LabelCatVotes.Text block to the first socket of __ + __.

19. Add 0

Click on Math and click and drag the 0 block to the second socket of __ + __. Change the "0" value to a "1".

Now test it out and see if the cat meows and the votes change!

20. Repeat for ButtonDog

Add when ButtonCat.Click, connect call PlayerCat.Start. Next, add set LabelCatVotes.Text to, add __ + __ to that block and connect LabelCatVotes.Text to the first socket and 0 to the second socket (and make sure to change the "0" to a "1".

Now test it out and see if the cat meows and the votes change!

8. Test your App!

When you're done test your app out on your device and collect votes from your friends!


Expand your app

When your app works, try some of these expansions:

  • What would we have to add so that we can click a button and “reset” the votes back to zero?
  • What are some other ways we could increment CatVote and DogVote?
  • Could we use one player instead of two players to play the sounds? What else in the code would have to change if we used two players?
  • Is there anything to stop someone from voting 20 times?
  • Change the app to be about something else other than voting on cats or dogs. What else could an app like this be used for?
