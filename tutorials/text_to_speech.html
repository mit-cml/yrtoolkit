Text-to-Speech

1. Introduction

Get started with App Inventor by creating an app that translates text to speech!

Connect using the companion app to test your work on your mobile device. If you don't know how to connect, click here.

1. Add a button

Find the Button Component in the User Interface section of your Pallete. Drag it onto your Viewer. A new button will now appear on the Viewer.

Check to see if you can see your button in your companion app.

2. Change button text

In the properties panel, change the text for Button1. Under the Text property, replace the existing text with "Talk To Me".

Notice that the text on your app's button changes right away.

3. Add TextToSpeech componenet

Go to the Media drawer in the Palette.

Find the TextToSpeech component. Drag and drop it onto your Viewer. It will appear under "Non-visible components."

4. Add Button1 click handler

It's time to tell your app what to do. The Blocks Editor is where you program the behavior of your app. Click the button "Blocks" to move over to the Blocks Editor.

Click on Button1 under Screen1.

Choose when Button1.Click.

Drag the block to the workspace.

5. Add TextToSpeech "Speak" procedure

Click on TextToSpeech.

Drag out and connect "call TextToSpeech1.Speak".

5. Add Text blocks

In Built-in blocks, click Text.

Drag out an empty quotes ("") block.

Enter whatever text you want to be spoken into the empty string.

5. Test your app!

Can you make your app speak when the button is pressed?

Next, try the "Text-to-Speech Expansion" tutorial or any app of your own! Use the how-tos if you get stuck on something.
