About this Tutorial

This tutorial is part of the Personal Image Classifier tutorials. In this tutorial, you will use the model you trained in Personal Image Classifier Part 1 in a mobile app that will identify objects based on your model. Below is a demo of the app you will build.

Connect your App

If you need help getting started and set up with App Inventor, please visit our Setting Up App Inventor page.

Personal Image Classifier Tutorial

1. Introduction

The components for this app have all been pre-built for you. What you will do is load the model you created in Part 1 of this tutorial, using the classifier.appinventor.mit.edu website.

On the Designer screen, look at the components included in the app.


2. Add your Model

Click on the PersonalImageClassifier1 component in the Components list. Then click on Model in the Properties pane, and click on model.mdl. Choose Upload File, locate the model file you downloaded in Part 1 of the tutorial, and upload that file as the new model.


3. Download and Install the App

Under the Build menu, choose "App (provide QR code for .apk)" to build the apk file to install on your mobile device. Once the apk is built, scan the QR code with a QR Code Scanner app on your device. If you don't have a QR Code Scanner app installed, either download one from the Play Store, or use the MIT AI Companion to scan and download the apk. Once downloaded, follow the instructions to install the app.

4. Update the App Settings

When you first run the app, it will ask for Camera and Storage permissions. Go to the Settings on your device, and make sure Camera and Storage permissions are turned on for the app.

5. Try out the app!

Run the app and try identifying some expressions with the app. Note there are two ways to classify in the app. If you click the Classify Image button, the Camera app on your device opens. You may need to toggle the camera to face you in the Camera app. Click on the checkmark once you take your picture and it should return the classification in the PIC app.

If you click the Classify Video button, it automatically shows the video within the app. If you click the button again, the app will classify what currently appears in the Webviewer window.

How accurate is your model? Does it identify your different expressions correctly? If not, you might want to go back and train your model more at classifier.appinventor.mit.edu.
