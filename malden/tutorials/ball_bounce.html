Ball Bounce

1. Introduction

In this tutorial, you will learn about animation in App Inventor by making a Ball (a sprite) bounce around on the screen (on a Canvas).

Connect using the companion app so you can test this on your mobile device. If you don't know how to connect, click the Connect your App tab above.

2. Add a Canvas

Click on the Drawing and Animation drawer and drag a Canvas component to your viewer.

3. Set Height and Width to "Fill parent"

In the Properties panel, click Height and set it to "Fill parent". Do the same with the Width.

4. Add a Ball

Click on the Drawing and Animation drawer again and drag out a Ball component onto the Canvas.

5. Set Radius to 15

In the Properties panel, type "15" into the Radius texbox. This will make the ball easier to fling.

6. Add when Ball1.Flung

Click the Blocks button to switch to the Blocks Editor.

Click Ball1 and click and drag when Ball1.Flung to the Viewer.

7. Set Heading for Ball1 based on flinging action

Click on Ball1 again. Click and drag the set Ball1.Heading to block and add it to when Ball1.Flung.

Go to the when Ball1.Flung block. Hover over "heading" and click on get heading. Add it to the set Ball1.Heading to block.

This sets the heading, or direction of the ball according to the flinging action.

8. Set Speed for Ball1

Let's add blocks to make the ball move.

Get the set Ball1.Speed to block and place it in the flung event.

Go to the when Ball1.Flung block. Hover over "speed" and click on get speed. Add it to the set Ball1.Speed to block.

Then test it out on your device. Fling the ball and it should move in the direction of your fling. You'll notice that it gets "stuck" on the edge of the screen though. Next, you'll add an event to make the ball bounce off the edge of the screen.

9. When Ball1 reaches an edge, make it bounce.

Click on Ball1 again and drag out the when Ball1.EdgeReached block.

Click on Ball1 again and connect the call Ball1.Bounce block to when Ball1.EdgeReached.

Hover over "edge" and click on get edge. Add it to the call Ball1.Bounce block.

Then test it out on your device. Your ball should bounce off the edges.

10. Extend your App!

After testing your app, try to extend your app using these suggestions or come up with your own!

  • Change the color of the ball based on how fast it is moving or which edge it reaches
  • Adjust the speed of the ball so that it slows down and stops after it gets flung
  • Give the ball obstacles or targets to hit
  • Introduce a paddle for intercepting the ball, like a Pong game

Don't forget to complete the Ball Bounce check-in questions.
