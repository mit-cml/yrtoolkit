Talk to Me Tutorial

Introduction

In this tutorial, you will learn to make an app that speaks words you type in aloud!

Connect using the companion app so you can test this on your mobile device. Start by launching the companion app on your phone or tablet.

Connect to AI Companion

Select "AI Companion" under Connect in App Inventor

Scan QR Code

Click on "scan QR code" on the companion App (on your phone or tablet)

Scan QR code with camera

Then, hold your tablet's camera up to the QR code (the black and white square) that appears on your computer screen.

You're now connected!

Add a Button

Under User Interface, drag button to the screen.

Change the text on the button to "Talk to Me"

Click on the button to view its Properties in the window on the right of the screen. "Text" is towards the bottom.

Add text-to-speech capabilities

From the Media drawer, drag TextToSpeech to the screen.

Switch to the blocks editor

Click on the Blocks button in the top right of your screen.

Make a button click event

On the left panel, click on Button1 to see the available blocks. Drag the when Button1.Click block to the workspace.

Program the TextToSpeech action

From the TextToSpeech1 component drawer (1), drag the call TextToSpeech1.Speak block (2) into the when Button1.Click block (3).

When you connect the blocks, they should click into place - if you have your speakers on, you can hear the clicking sound!

Fill in the message socket

Drag an empty Text block from the Text drawer and connect it to the message slot in the call TextToSpeech1.Speak block.

Enter whatever message you want the app to say when the button is clicked.

Test out your app!

On your device, press the button and you should hear your message being spoken. You might have to reconnect the companion app.

Part 2: Say anything

Go back to the Designer.

And drag a TextBox onto the screen from the User Interface drawer and drop it onto the screen above the Button.

Change the hint text

Click on TextBox1, and in its Properties window, change the Hint property to something like "enter some text".

Change the message to what the user types in the TextBox

Switch back to Blocks Editor.

Remove the message you had in the call TextToSpeech1.Speak message slot, and drag a TextBox1.Text block from the TextBox1 Blocks drawer (you will need to scroll down to see it).

Test again!

Test the app on your device. You should now be able to type something in the text box, then press the button, and the app will speak the message.

Part 3: Shake It (Challenge!)

First, check to make sure your device has an accelerometer sensor.

You'll add a feature so the app says something whenever you shake it.

The accelerometer is a device on your phone or tablet that senses when the device has been moved quickly (like being shaken)!

To start, go back to the Designer View.

Add an accelerometer sensor

The accelerometer sensor is in the Sensors drawer.

Add an accelerometer shaking event

Go back to the Blocks Editor, and drag a when AccelerometerSensor1.Shaking block into the workspace.

Make the app speak when you shake the device

Copy the call TextToSpeech1.Speak block from when Button1.Click and snap it into the when AccelerometerSensor1.Shaking block.

Note: In App Inventor, you can copy any blocks by right clicking and selecting "Duplicate".

Remove the TextBox1.Text block. Drag in a blank Text block and change it to "Stop Shaking Me!"

Test it out! If you shake the device, it should say "Stop Shaking Me!"

Sometimes if you haven't used the companion in a while it can disconnect. Repeat the connection process from Step 1 if it disconnects.

Congratulations! You've completed the app.
